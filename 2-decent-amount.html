Decent Amount of Almost

K.L. Abecia

1665

"Decent Amount of Almost" is a captivating painting that features two divided portraits, one of a contemplative man and the other of a resilient woman. Despite their physical separation on the canvas, a palpable connection exists between them, hinting at an unseen force that binds their worlds. The artist skillfully leaves the narrative open-ended, inviting viewers to contemplate the delicate balance between dreams and reality. The title suggests a moment suspended in time, where the future is both imminent and elusive, and the canvas becomes a portal to the "almost" that lingers between what is and what could be.
